Music

EDM is short for electronic dance music which is the form of music that involves electronic equipment like synthesizers, drum machines, processed samples and recordings. These songs are electronic in nature which can be easily identified after hearing to it.

History of EDM

The EDM emerged when the early synthesizers were created and was inspired from disco genre. One of the first types of EDM was House music. After the invention of computers EDM became more popular. It picked up the pace starting from early 2000s. The EDM producers were heavily influenced by the invention of the synthesizer in 1965. Soon all the artist started using synthesizer in their songs and the genre of EDM would cease to exist without it.

Personal computers in EDM

The personal computer changed a lot of things for EDM artist. Everyone had to have a computer to play their sets in clubs or concerts, maintain their social media profiles (Twitter, Facebook, Instagram) to better market themselves which all was possible due to the emergence of personal computer. It made some of the automation procedures easy to accomplish. Computer music technology has emerged as one of the key tools for contemporary music education.

Auto-Tune

When vocalists sing out of tune, whether live or in the studio, Auto-Tune is a piece of software that corrects them. No one can sing perfectly during a live concert hence the invention of Auto-Tune helps the artists to fix pitch-faulty notes to be instantly and discreetly tweaked to the nearest semitone, making for pitch-perfect vocal performances every time. Some of the notable artists using Auto-Tune are T-pain and Travis Scott
